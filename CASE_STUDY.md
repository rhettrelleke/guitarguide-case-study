# Case Study

## The Product Problem
Chord-based songwriting often starts with something small: a shape, a color, a tension, or a progression fragment that feels worth following. The problem is not usually finding a first idea. The problem is keeping momentum long enough to turn that idea into something usable.

Many players patch together that process with separate tools. One place shows chord shapes. Another stores notes. Another holds voice memos. That fragmentation creates friction at the exact moment when creative momentum matters most.

GuitarGuide is a response to that friction.

## The Product Bet
The product makes a clear bet: a focused tool for chord exploration and progression building can be more useful than a broader tool that tries to do everything around music.

That bet shaped the product in two ways:
- it narrowed scope aggressively
- it prioritized movement, clarity, and evaluation over feature breadth

## What This Repository Is Demonstrating
This case study is not meant to explain how the app was built step by step. It is meant to show what kinds of decisions shaped it.

The useful questions are:
- what problem was considered worth solving
- what tradeoffs were accepted to keep the product clear
- what standards were applied to UX and product scope
- what engineering judgment is visible through those choices

## Key Constraints
- The product needed to feel practical for real writing sessions, not just impressive in a feature list.
- The experience needed to support exploration without becoming cluttered.
- Alternate tunings needed to feel native to the product direction.
- Free users needed a real workflow, not a teaser.
- Public materials about the product needed to show seriousness without exposing the internal implementation too directly.

## Product Decisions That Mattered

### Focus Over Breadth
Rather than expanding into every adjacent music use case, GuitarGuide keeps its center of gravity around chords, progressions, playback, and writing flow.

This makes the product easier to understand and more opinionated in use.

### Evaluation Over Reference
Reference alone is not enough for songwriting. The product treats hearing and comparing ideas as part of the core workflow because players need to judge what a progression does, not just identify what it is.

### Workflow Over Feature Count
Features matter less than whether the tool helps someone stay in motion. The product favors decisions that reduce switching, preserve context, and keep the next step close at hand.

### Practical Depth Over Surface Complexity
Depth is valuable when it supports real use. The product tries to add expressive range where it helps players write better, not where it only makes the app sound more advanced.

## What This Shows About Engineering Judgment
The product required technical decisions, but the more important signal is how those decisions were bounded.

The public-facing story here is not "we built many systems." It is "we made deliberate tradeoffs to support a specific creative workflow."

That kind of restraint is part of engineering judgment too.

## What Was Intentionally Left Out
This repository does not document implementation sequences, internal architecture, proprietary suggestion logic, monetization mechanics, or analytics details.

That omission is deliberate. The value of this repo comes from clarity and selectivity, not from completeness.
