# Principles

## 1. Speed To Musical Feedback Matters
The product should help players hear whether an idea works as quickly as possible. Delay breaks confidence and weakens creative momentum.

## 2. The Interface Should Protect Flow
The best writing tools do not demand attention from the user. They stay out of the way while still making the next useful action obvious.

## 3. Chords Are More Useful In Context
A voicing becomes more meaningful when it can be tried inside a progression, heard back, and compared against another direction.

## 4. Focus Is A Product Advantage
The product should resist becoming a general-purpose music app. A narrower promise makes the experience sharper and easier to trust.

## 5. Tuning Is Not A Niche Edge Case
For many guitar players, tuning changes the entire writing experience. The product should treat that context as part of the workflow, not as an afterthought.

## 6. Suggestions Should Feel Intentional
Guidance should help the player move forward without feeling noisy or exhaustive. The goal is confidence, not volume.

## 7. Useful Free Products Build Better Trust
The free experience should be genuinely valuable on its own. Premium should deepen the workflow, not rescue a weak product.
